UNESCO World Heritage

Okavango Delta

The Okavango Delta is the world's largest inland delta and one of Africa's greatest wildlife sanctuaries. Every year, floodwaters from Angola transform this Kalahari desert region into a lush, labyrinthine wetland.

Mokoro Canoe Rides Big Five Game Viewing Luxury Island Camps Birdwatching Paradise

Elephant Capital

Chobe National Park

Chobe is home to Africa's largest elephant population — over 50,000 elephants. Iconic river boat safaris along the Chobe River offer unforgettable wildlife encounters at every turn.

Elephant Herds River Boat Safaris Lion & Leopard Chobe Riverfront
Book This Destination
Salt & Stars

Makgadikgadi Pans

One of the world's largest salt flats, Makgadikgadi is an otherworldly landscape of white salt, ancient baobabs, and Africa's second-largest zebra migration. At night, the Milky Way reflection is simply magical.

Zebra Migration Stargazing at Night Flamingo Colonies Quad Biking
Book This Destination
First Wildlife Reserve

Moremi Game Reserve

Moremi was Botswana's first wildlife reserve, covering one-third of the Okavango Delta. It offers classic African safari scenery of floodplains, mopane woodland and dense riverine forest.

Wild Dog Sightings Hippo & Croc Floodplain Drives Camping Under Stars
Book This Destination
Africa's Largest Reserve

Central Kalahari

The Central Kalahari Game Reserve is the second-largest game reserve in the world. Its vast red dunes, fossil riverbeds and ancient baobab trees make it an awe-inspiring and remote destination.

Black-Maned Lions Fossil Riverbeds Remote Wilderness San Bushmen Culture

Capital City

Gaborone City

Botswana's vibrant capital city blends modern development with traditional culture. Explore bustling markets, excellent museums, fine dining and the warm hospitality Batswana are famed for.
